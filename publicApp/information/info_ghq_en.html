We have asked participants twelve questions regarding their mental well-being.

Based on these questions, we have computed the GHQ12 score, which represents, on a scale of 12 points, the mental health of a person (0: no complaints, 12: serious mental complaints). These questions were previously asked in the Belgian Health questionnaire 2018 by Sciensano. At that time, the average Belgian had a score of 1.7 out of 12.

You can choose the population group for which you want to see the results.
We observe that the GHQ12-score has increased strongly during the corona epidemic.
Especially children and adolescents (0-17 year) and women have lower scores than under normal circumstances.

The 12 questions that were used to calculate the GHQ12 score, are:
  • Have you been able to concentrate on your activities during the past week?
  • Have you experienced insomnia because of worrying during the last week?
  • Have you felt that you were spending your time meaningfully this past week?
  • Have you felt able to make decisions about things during the past week?
  • Have you felt under constant pressure during the past week?
  • Have you felt like you could not handle your problems during the last week?
  • Have you been able to enjoy your normal, everyday activities during the past week?
  • Have you been able to face your problems this past week?
  • Have you felt unhappy or depressed during the last week?
  • Have you lost faith in yourself during the last week?
  • Have you felt like a useless person during the last week?
  • Have you felt reasonably happy during the last week, all things considered?
